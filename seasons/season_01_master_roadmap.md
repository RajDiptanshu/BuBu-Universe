# Season 01 Master Roadmap

**Status:** Strategic planning document — **roadmap only, not episode scripts.** Every episode below must still be built using the full `canon/bubu_project_canon.md` Episode Creation Rule (all 6 canon layers) at the time it is actually produced. This document exists to keep that production aligned with `canon/bubu_franchise_vision.md` from Episode 2 onward.

**Source canon:** `bubu_core_identity_block.md`, `bubu_personality_bible.md`, `bubu_universe_bible.md`, `content_pillars.md`, `engagement_framework.md`, `visual_style_guide.md`, `bubu_franchise_vision.md`.

## 1. Season Thesis

Season 1 builds Bubu into a recognizable preschool IP before expanding into Hindi rhymes or side characters. By Episode 100, a repeat viewer should be able to recognize Bubu, anticipate the rhythm of every episode, recognize the Bubu world's 10 locations, and feel the first gentle layer of Hindi bilingual content arrive without any disruption to the format they already trust. Season 1 is the foundation the rest of the franchise (Hindi feed, long-form compilations, Friendship Pond cast expansion, merchandise) is built on top of — every episode in this roadmap should be checkable against `bubu_franchise_vision.md`'s 5-year picture, not just against this season's goals.

---

## 2. Season Guardrails

These rules apply to every episode in this roadmap and may not be relaxed for an individual episode's sake:

1. **Bubu remains the sole mascot for the majority of Season 1.** No named recurring side character is introduced. Where the Animal Friends pillar (`content_pillars.md` #5) is used, the "friend" is a simple, unnamed, geometric animal-shaped payoff object — not a recurring character — per Universe Bible's constraint that Friendship Pond's cast is reserved for later.
2. **English-first for the initial batch.** Episodes 1–60 (Arcs 1–3) are English-only, establishing the core format, vocabulary, and signatures before any second language is layered in.
3. **Hindi words are introduced gradually, as a layer — not as a new format.** Starting in Arc 4 (Episodes 61–80), individual Hindi words are paired with English words the audience has *already learned* in Arcs 1–3 (colors, numbers, routine verbs). Hindi is never the primary teaching vocabulary of a *new* concept in Season 1 — it reinforces concepts already established in English. "Arre, Bubu!" is the one exception: it is Hindi/Indian-English from Episode 1 onward, unchanged, per `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §3–4.
4. **Side characters and new locations (Friendship Pond) are delayed until the universe is established.** Friendship Pond may only be *visually teased* (distant view, no character shown) in the final arc, after all other 9 locations have been used at least once. No named Animal Friend, and no full introduction at Friendship Pond, occurs in Season 1 — that is reserved for the long-form expansion trigger point described in `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §6.
5. **Every episode must reinforce the Daily Wonder Cycle and Wonder Pebbles.** Every episode's Payoff beat must create or interact with a Wonder Pebble (directly, per World Rule #8, or by visiting Pebble Hill). No episode may treat its payoff as disconnected from this central continuity mechanic.
6. **The 5-beat structure, world rules, and locked v2 identity are never altered.** This roadmap adds variety only through pillar, location, keyword, hero color, and payoff object — never through new beats, new expressions, new trunk shapes, or new background complexity.

## 3. Arc Overview

Season 1 is divided into five 20-episode arcs, each with a distinct focus while sharing the same underlying grammar:

| Arc | Episodes | Focus | Primary Locations Introduced/Used |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1–20 | **Bubu identity / discovery** — establishing Bubu's signatures, home, favorite thing, and the Wonder Pebble mechanic | The Nook, The Puddle, Pebble Hill (+ first visits to Color Garden, Shape Hill) |
| 2 | 21–40 | **Colors and shapes** — full color palette and shape vocabulary | The Color Garden, Shape Hill, Pebble Hill |
| 3 | 41–60 | **Nature and environment** — counting in nature, vehicles, playground, first Big Feelings/comfort episode | The Counting Tree, The Go-Go Trail, The Playground Mound, Whisper Grass |
| 4 | 61–80 | **Hindi first words / bilingual transition** — Hindi words layered onto already-learned English vocabulary | Revisits all locations established in Arcs 1–3, bilingual on-screen text |
| 5 | 81–100 | **Feelings, friendship, and gentle social interaction** — Animal Friends pillar debut, Friendship Pond tease, season-wide review | The Puddle, The Color Garden, Shape Hill, The Playground Mound, Whisper Grass, Friendship Pond (tease only), full-meadow recap |

## 6. Episode Dependency Notes

- **Pebble Hill (Ep 3) must exist before any "Pebble Hill grows" episode.** Episodes 17, 33, 59, 76, 79, 94, and 99 all depend on Pebble Hill having been established as a location and on at least one earlier episode having added a pebble — they show *cumulative* growth, which only means something if earlier pebbles exist.
- **The first comfort/fear episode (Ep 55) must precede every later Whisper Grass or "calm" episode.** Episodes 56, 74, 89, and 90 all build on the fear→resolution pattern established in Episode 55; none of them should air first, or the "always resolves" rule (World Rule #5) has nothing to resolve *from*.
- **Hindi words (Arc 4, Eps 61–80) must not appear before the matching English word has already been taught.** Each Hindi-word episode pairs a Hindi word with a concept from Arcs 1–3 (e.g., Ep 65 "Lal/Red" depends on Ep 13 "A Red Hello"; Ep 62 "Ek/One" depends on Ep 5 "Bubu's Pebble Pat"). Arc 4 cannot be reordered ahead of the arcs it reinforces.
- **Animal Friends episodes (Eps 81–86) should not appear before the locations they use are established.** Each depends on its location's earlier pillar episode(s) — e.g., Ep 85 "Splash Fish" depends on The Puddle already being familiar from Arc 1.
- **Friendship Pond may only be teased (Eps 91–92) after all other 9 locations have appeared at least once.** This is a hard ordering constraint, not a stylistic choice — per `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §6 and Universe Bible #6, Friendship Pond's first *real* use (a named side character) must wait for long-form, and even the *tease* should only land once the rest of the world feels complete.
- **No episode in Season 1 may introduce a named, recurring side character.** If a future planning pass wants to do this, it must happen as part of the long-form expansion described in `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §6, not by inserting an episode into this roadmap.

## 7. End-of-Season Outcome

By Episode 100, Season 1 should have achieved:

- **Bubu is instantly recognizable** — the Wobble, Ear-Perk & Point, the hook trunk, and "Arre, Bubu!" have each appeared in dozens of episodes, muted-legible from frame one.
- **The audience knows the world** — all 10 recurring locations (The Nook, The Puddle, Pebble Hill, The Color Garden, Shape Hill, The Counting Tree, The Go-Go Trail, The Playground Mound, Whisper Grass, and a first glimpse of Friendship Pond) have been visited.
- **Children can anticipate the rhythm** — the 5-beat structure, the "good things take three tries" pattern, and the Daily Wonder Cycle are familiar enough that repeat viewers feel "I knew that would happen!" (Universe Bible §8).
- **Hindi has been layered on without confusing the brand** — Arc 4 proves that Hindi words can sit alongside English vocabulary the audience already knows, setting up the dedicated Hindi feed from `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §3.
- **Pebble Hill visibly holds a season's worth of memory** — by Episode 99, Pebble Hill displays the accumulated record of (in-world) every wonder Bubu has found across the season, proving "the meadow remembers" (World Rule #8) at scale.
- **The universe is ready for Season 2** — Friendship Pond has been teased but not used, Animal Friends has been introduced as a pillar (via unnamed shapes) without a named character, and the franchise's long-form and side-character expansion triggers (per `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §6) are now ready to be pulled.
