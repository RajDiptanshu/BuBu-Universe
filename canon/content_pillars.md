# Content Pillars — Bubu Channel

8 pillars provide variety across 100+ episodes while every episode follows the same `character_bible.md`, `visual_style_guide.md`, and `engagement_framework.md`. Pillars are ordered roughly by ease-of-generation (easiest first), which guides pilot episode selection.

---

## 1. Water Play

- **Teaching goal:** Cause-and-effect (trunk action → water response), the word "splash"/"water," sensory vocabulary (wet, cool).
- **Typical objects:** A small puddle/pool shape, water droplets, a floating object (leaf, ball).
- **Payoff style:** Bubu's trunk fills and sprays — water arcs across frame, turns the hero color (bright blue/turquoise), droplets scatter. The single most "on-brand" payoff for an elephant mascot.
- **Visual dressing:** Ground layer becomes a flat water shape (still pastel/desaturated until payoff); sky/horizon unchanged.

## 2. Colors

- **Teaching goal:** Recognize and name one color per episode.
- **Typical objects:** One neutral-gray object (ball, balloon, flower bud) that Bubu pats/points at repeatedly.
- **Payoff style:** The neutral object suddenly fills with the hero color at full saturation (e.g., gray balloon → red).
- **Visual dressing:** Standard meadow base; no special dressing needed — easiest pillar to produce.

## 3. Shapes

- **Teaching goal:** Recognize one shape per episode (circle, square, triangle, star).
- **Typical objects:** A flat gray shape outline on the ground; Bubu traces it with trunk-point motions.
- **Payoff style:** The outline fills solid in the hero color and lifts/bounces once.
- **Visual dressing:** Standard meadow base; shape sits on the ground in Bubu's "object zone" (opposite third from Bubu).

## 4. Counting (1–3)

- **Teaching goal:** Numbers one, two, three; concept of "more."
- **Typical objects:** Identical small objects (pebbles, leaves, bubbles), appearing one at a time as Bubu pats/points once per object.
- **Payoff style:** Once all objects are present (2 or 3, matching Build repetitions), they all activate together — bounce, glow, or roll in sync.
- **Visual dressing:** Standard meadow base; objects line up along the ground between Bubu and frame edge.

## 5. Animal Friends

- **Teaching goal:** Animal names and sounds; social concept "friend"/"hello."
- **Typical objects:** One small friend-animal shape (simple geometric design matching Bubu's style — duck, frog, bird, etc.).
- **Payoff style:** The friend-animal performs its signature movement (hop, waddle, fly) toward Bubu, who responds with a trunk-hug/pat, both reacting in "Delight."
- **Visual dressing:** Standard meadow base; friend animal enters from the object-zone third.

## 6. Things That Go (Vehicles)

- **Teaching goal:** Vehicle names; concepts "fast"/"go."
- **Typical objects:** One small gray vehicle shape (car, truck, train segment) that Bubu pats/points at.
- **Payoff style:** The vehicle turns hero-color and zooms across frame in one fast directional motion, leaving a simple motion-streak.
- **Visual dressing:** Ground layer gains a short flat road-strip; sky/horizon unchanged.

## 7. Playground

- **Teaching goal:** Action verbs (climb, slide, swing, jump, roll); concepts "up"/"down."
- **Typical objects:** One simple playground element (slide shape, swing shape, low step shapes).
- **Payoff style:** Bubu performs the equipment's signature release motion (slides down, swings forward) — the fastest/largest Bubu-motion of any pillar, ending in "Delight."
- **Visual dressing:** Standard meadow base + one playground element as the secondary set-piece.

## 8. Daily Routines

- **Teaching goal:** Routine verbs (wash, brush, eat, sleep); concept "all done."
- **Typical objects:** One routine-related object (cup, toothbrush shape, blanket, bowl).
- **Payoff style:** Completing the routine action (3rd repetition) produces a small celebratory result (sparkle, soft glow, content sigh) — calmer/slower payoff than other pillars by design.
- **Visual dressing:** Ground/sky layers swap for a simple flat room-interior (same horizon-line composition, room-toned colors).

---

## Pillar Generation-Ease Ranking (for pilot selection)

| Rank | Pillar | Why it's easy/hard |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (easiest) | Colors | Single static object, one color-fill transform — minimal motion |
| 2 | Shapes | Single static object, one fill+bounce — minimal motion |
| 3 | Counting | Repetition of one simple object, synced group motion at payoff |
| 4 | Water Play | Requires water/particle effects — slightly higher AI-gen complexity, but highest brand value |
| 5 | Animal Friends | Requires a second character design, two-character interaction |
| 6 | Things That Go | Requires fast directional motion + motion streak |
| 7 | Daily Routines | Requires room-interior background (departure from default world) |
| 8 (hardest) | Playground | Requires the largest Bubu-motion (slide/swing) — highest motion-consistency risk |
