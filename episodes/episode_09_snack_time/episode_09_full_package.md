## 3. Video Prompts

Static, locked-off 9:16 camera throughout — no pans, tilts, zooms, or cuts. Image-to-video from each scene's keyframe; motion only.

1. **Hello (2s):** Gentle idle Wobble; corner pebble and wash-bowl glow with a slow, soft steady pulse. In the final frames, Bubu's body begins leaning forward and the trunk begins curling toward the snack-bowl.
2. **Build (4s):** The trunk curls beneath the bowl, lifts it a small distance off the ground and holds it close to the chest, then sets it back down — repeated three times, evenly spaced (~1.3s each), each lift identical in height and speed. The background glows remain steady. After the third lift, the trunk lifts toward vertical and the body straightens.
3. **Anticipation (2s):** Near-freeze. The bowl rests on the ground, perfectly still and pale. Bubu holds upright, trunk straight up, ears popped outward; only a slow blink and faint breathing motion.
4. **Payoff (3s):** In the first ~0.5s the bowl rapidly brightens from pale gray-blue to fully saturated warm peach, with a few sparkle flecks rising above it; the corner pebble and wash-bowl both brighten in the same beat. Simultaneously Bubu hops once, ears flap outward with a small contented sigh, trunk swings up and back. Over the remaining ~2.5s the sparkles fade and everything settles.
5. **Loop (1s, generated at 2s minimum then trimmed):** Bubu eases from the Delight hop back into its calm Scene-1 resting pose and trunk curl; the snack-bowl settles to a soft steady peach glow and the corner pebble and wash-bowl return to their steady established glows. End frame should closely match Scene 1's opening frame except for the snack-bowl's new glow.

## 5. Educational Objective

Introduces the routine word "Eat" and the result word "Yum," modeling a calmer, gentler pacing for the Daily Wonder Cycle's third step ("Snack time in The Nook") and demonstrating the trunk's "Hold" function — lifting and bringing an object close — as a new gentle action distinct from Point, Tap, and Pat.

## 6. Brand-Building Objective

Establishes Daily Routines' calmer payoff pacing (slower percussion, contented sigh) as a recognizable tonal variation within the format, while the Episode-02 pebble's and Episode-06 wash-bowl's brightening cameos show viewers that The Nook's glow-objects accumulate and respond together — reinforcing "the meadow remembers" (World Rule #8) with a third object in a single recurring location.
