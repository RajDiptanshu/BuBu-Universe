## 1. Episode Brief

**One-line concept:** Daily Routines pillar, The Nook, Bubu's trunk taps three times toward a gap in the leaf-roof — and morning light *Pops!* into the Nook for the first time, lighting up Bubu Grove's very first Wonder Pebble.

**Why this episode, why now:** Episode 02 is the franchise's **second-ever episode** and the first to operate under the full Episode 02+ canon update — mandatory Scene 0 Brand Sting, Wonder Pebble visible from Scene 1, the turquoise discovery flash, the vibrancy-bumped palette, and the Phase 1 core vocabulary. Per the Named Pebble Cadence Rule (`canon/wonder_pebble_taxonomy.md`), Episode 02 claims the **First Wonder Pebble** (Flagship #1) — the pebble that *introduces the Wonder Pebble mechanic itself*. Pairing that mechanic-introduction with a "waking up" story is a deliberate fit: the very first thing a returning viewer learns is "small gray things in Bubu Grove can hide something wonderful," at the same moment Bubu itself is waking up to a new day — mechanic and story reinforce each other.

**The four "must decide before generating anything" items** (Step 2):
| Item | Decision |
|---|---|
| Pillar | Daily Routines (different from Episode 01's Water Play — satisfies rotation, not blocks) |
| Hero color | Sunrise Cream `#FFE3A8` — warm, distinct from Episode 01's turquoise hero color and from the franchise-fixed Wonder Pebble turquoise `#4FD1C5`; opens the hero-color non-repetition window |
| Build word | "Tap!" (Phase 1 core) |
| Payoff word | "Pop!" (Phase 1 core) |

**Trunk function used:** Point (Bubu's trunk points/taps toward the leaf-roof light-gap) — one of the four canonical trunk functions (`canon/bubu_personality_bible.md`).

## 3. Emotional Arc

Bubu's emotional journey follows the franchise-constant **Wonder → Focus → (brief) Anticipation → Joy → Wonder** arc (`canon/bubu_personality_bible.md` #3, "Joyful Wonder"), mapped to this episode's "waking up" story:

| Beat | Emotional state | What it feels like | Canon anchor |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 (Sting) | Baseline recognition | "There's Bubu" — calm, familiar, before the story even starts | BUBU! Brand Sting (`bubu_personality_bible.md` #7) |
| 1 (Hello) | **Quiet wonder** | Bubu wakes in the dim, calm Nook — gentle curiosity, nothing urgent yet | World Rule #1 ("one quiet world") |
| 2 (Build) | **Focused effort** | Three identical, calm taps toward the light — concentration, not excitement; the *only* asymmetric expression (one eyebrow slightly lowered) | World Rule #3 ("good things take three tries") |
| 3 (Anticipation) | **Held breath** | Everything stops; ears pop in surprise; the dim light holds at its brightest pale level — the episode's lowest-energy, most still moment | World Rule #4 ("the world holds its breath") |
| 4 (Payoff) | **Bright joy** | The single loudest, brightest, fastest moment of the episode — light floods in, Bubu hops, "Arre, Bubu! ...Pop!" | World Rule #1 ("one loud payoff"); Personality Bible "Joyful Wonder" |
| 5 (Loop) | **Settled wonder** | Energy eases back to Scene 1's calm — but the world is now subtly different (the pebble glows), inviting the next watch | World Rule #8 ("the meadow remembers") |

**Why this arc fits "Good Morning, Bubu":** the Wonder→Joy→Wonder shape *is* the shape of waking up — calm, a little effort, a held-breath moment before the day "arrives," then delight, settling back into calm. No new emotional beat is introduced; this episode simply finds a story where the existing arc is the most natural possible fit (Production Philosophy: "Character-First, Not Rhyme-First" — `production/production_bible.md` §1).

**Mandatory chant vocals (Audio Evolution Rule, World Rule #10):** per Producer Review Adjustment 5, this episode **requires** a rhythmic chant mixed softly into the Build-beat music bed — *"Tap tap tap... ## 7. Loop Strategy

- **Visual loop point:** Scene 5's trimmed final frame must closely match Scene 1's opening frame — same Bubu pose (Curious, trunk at 0°), same dim Nook lighting, same camera framing — with exactly **one** intentional difference: the Wonder Pebble retains a faint turquoise glow in Scene 5, where it was plain pale gray-blue in Scene 1. This is the episode's "the meadow remembers" signal (World Rule #8) and does not disrupt the loop's visual continuity, since it's a small, low-saturation detail in the corner of frame, not the focal point.
- **On-screen text reset:** The key word "Tap!" disappears by the end of Scene 2 and does not reappear in Scenes 3-5, so the Scene 5 → Scene 1 restart reads as a clean reset, not a repeated label (`canon/engagement_framework.md`, "How the Loop Works").
- **Audio loop point:** The music bed settles back to its 20% base level by the end of Scene 5, exactly matching its Scene-1 starting level — the Beat 5 → Beat 1 loop is audio-seamless as well as visually seamless.
- **No transition effects:** the loop (and every other scene-to-scene join) is a hard cut only — continuity is solved entirely at the keyframe level (Step 6), per `production/production_bible.md` §5.
- **Scene 0 is outside the loop:** the Brand Sting is prepended once via concat at export (`production/production_bible.md` Step 9) and is not part of the Scene 5 → Scene 1 replay loop — repeated plays of the looping Short replay Scenes 1-5 only, with the sting having already played once at the start of the viewing session.
- **Total exported runtime:** 0.5s (Scene 0) + 12s (Scenes 1-5) = **12.5s**, within the mandatory 10.5-15.5s range (`canon/engagement_framework.md` Beat 0). The Producer Review re-balance (Anticipation 2.0s → 1.5s, Payoff 3.0s → 3.5s) shifts time *within* Scenes 1-5 only — the 12s total, and therefore the loop point itself, is unchanged.
