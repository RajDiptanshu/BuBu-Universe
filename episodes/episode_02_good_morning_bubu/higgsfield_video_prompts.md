# Episode 02 — "Good Morning, Bubu!" — Higgsfield Video Prompts

One Higgsfield image-to-video prompt per scene, generated from that scene's approved keyframe in [`image_prompts.md`](image_prompts.md). Per the project rule, **image prompts define appearance; these prompts define motion only.** Every clip must be generated with the same Character Lock reference (`Bubu_Master_v1` + `Bubu_ThreeQuarter_v1` + the scene's expression asset) supplied alongside the keyframe, at high identity strength, so Bubu's proportions/palette/outline never drift frame-to-frame.

> **Shared settings across all 6 scenes:**
> - Static, locked-off 9:16 camera. No pans, no tilts, no zooms, no dolly, no handheld shake, no rotation.
> - No new characters, no on-screen text generated by the video model (text is added in editing per `editing_plan.md`).
> - No new background elements, no new objects, no accessory changes.
> - Bubu's body palette (`#A9B7C4` / `#EAEFF2` / `#F4B8C0` / `#FBC7C7` / `#2B2B2B` / `#FFFFFF`) and the `#6B7C8C` outline never shift, brighten, or desaturate — color changes are reserved exclusively for the Wonder Pebble and the Sunrise Cream light-flood, per their scene-specific specs below.
> - Motion stays slow, toddler-readable, slow-in/slow-out — no snapping, no jitter, no motion blur.

## Scene 0 — BUBU! Brand Sting (Curious)

**3. Subject motion:** Continuous gentle Wobble idle loop (small side-to-side weight shift + soft ear sway), trunk and eyes otherwise still. No expression change, no trunk-rotation change.

**4. Motion intensity:** 2/10 — barely-perceptible idle motion only.

**5. Duration target:** Generate at minimum supported duration (typically 2s); trim to final **0.5s** in editing. This single approved clip becomes the byte-identical evergreen asset (`production/assets/bubu_brand_sting.mp4`), reused unmodified at the start of every future episode.

**6. Things that must remain locked:**
- Bubu's full v2 silhouette: teardrop body, two perfectly circular ~40%-width disc ears, uniform hook trunk (never tapering/pointed), four stubby cylinder legs, small rounded tail.

- Trunk at its 0° resting orientation throughout — no rotation drift.
- Generic shared-world background — no Nook elements, no Wonder Pebble, no pillar-specific dressing introduced by the video model.
- Composition: Bubu at 40-55% frame height, eyes ~40% from top, center-left, 9:16.

## Cross-Scene Consistency Notes

- Always supply the **same Character Lock reference set** (`Bubu_Master_v1` + `Bubu_ThreeQuarter_v1` + the scene's expression asset) as identity/character reference alongside each scene's starting keyframe, at identity strength ~0.8 — never let the video model's own interpretation override the locked silhouette, palette, or outline.
- Motion strength stays low (1-4/10) for every scene except Scene 4 (6/10) — Scene 4 is the episode's only high-energy beat by design (World Rule #1, "one quiet world, one loud payoff").
- If any clip drifts Bubu's proportions, ear shape, trunk shape, palette, or outline during motion, regenerate at a **lower** motion-strength value before adjusting prompt wording — never compensate for drift by editing the prompt's identity description.
- The Wonder Pebble's color is the **only** element permitted to change hue across the episode (pale gray-blue → tiny sparkle → turquoise flash → settled turquoise glow → faint persistent glow). Bubu's palette and the Nook's structural geometry never change hue, only saturation/brightness within their locked bands.
- None of these prompts introduce on-screen text, sound, or new background elements — those are handled entirely in `editing_plan.md`.
