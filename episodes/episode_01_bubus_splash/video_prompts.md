# Episode 01 — "Bubu's Splash" — Video Prompts

One motion-only prompt per scene, in three tool-specific variants (Higgsfield, Kling, Veo). Each variant should be run **image-to-video**, using that scene's approved keyframe from `image_prompts.md` as the starting frame — appearance, palette, and pose are already locked by the image; these prompts describe **motion only**, per the project rule that image prompts define appearance and video prompts define movement.

> **Shared settings across all scenes/tools:**
> - Static, locked-off 9:16 camera. No pans, no tilts, no zooms, no dolly, no handheld shake.
> - No new characters, no text overlays generated by the video model (text is added in editing).
> - Keep motion gentle and toddler-readable — slow in/slow out, no snapping or jitter.

---

## Scene 1 — Hello (0:00 – 0:02, 2s)

**Motion:** Gentle idle sway (gentle weight shift side-to-side, ears softly swaying), then in the final frames Bubu's body begins leaning forward and the trunk begins lifting toward the puddle — seamless lead-in to Scene 2's tap motion. Puddle surface stays calm, no ripples.

**Higgsfield:**
```
Image-to-video from the Scene 1 keyframe. Motion strength: 3/10 (subtle).
Bubu gently sways side to side in an idle "curious" loop — small weight
shift, ears softly swaying with the motion, trunk relaxed. In the last
third of the clip, Bubu's body begins a slow forward lean and the trunk
begins lifting toward the puddle on the right. Camera: locked static,
no movement, no zoom. Background: completely still, no wind/particle
effects. Puddle: perfectly still, no ripples. Duration: 2s.
```

**Kling:**
```
Image-to-video, camera control: static (no camera motion).
Motion strength: low-medium. Subject: Bubu (baby elephant) performs a
gentle idle sway — soft side-to-side weight shift and ear movement —
then begins leaning forward with the trunk lifting toward the puddle on
the lower right by the end of the clip. Background and puddle remain
static with no ripple until the very last frame. Smooth, slow, toddler-
friendly easing — no sudden motion. 2 seconds.
```

**Veo:**
```
A static, locked-off camera with absolutely no camera movement, no pans,
no zooms, no handheld motion. The baby elephant character gently sways
side to side in place — a soft, slow idle motion. Toward the end of the
shot, the character leans its body slightly forward and begins raising
its trunk toward a small puddle in the lower right of the frame. The
background and puddle stay completely still. Calm, slow, gentle physical
motion only — no camera effects, no cuts. 2 seconds.
```

## Scene 2 — Build (0:02 – 0:06, 4s)

**Motion:** Bubu leans forward and taps the curled trunk tip down onto the puddle surface three times in a steady, evenly-spaced rhythm. Each tap produces one small ripple ring that expands and fades before the next tap. After the third tap, Bubu pulls the trunk back and the body straightens, setting up Scene 3's stillness.

**Higgsfield:**
```
Image-to-video from the Scene 2 keyframe. Motion strength: 4/10.
Bubu's trunk performs three repeated downward taps onto the puddle
surface, evenly spaced across the clip (roughly one tap per 1.2-1.3s).
Each tap triggers one small circular ripple that expands outward and
fades before the next tap begins. Body stays leaning forward and mostly
still between taps — only the trunk moves. After the third tap, the
trunk lifts back and the body straightens upright in the final frames.
Camera: locked static, no movement. Background: still. Duration: 4s.
```

**Kling:**
```
Image-to-video, camera control: static (no camera motion).
Motion strength: medium, localized to the trunk. Subject: Bubu's trunk
taps the puddle surface three times in a steady repeating rhythm, evenly
spaced through the clip. Each tap produces a single small ripple ring on
the water that expands and settles before the next tap. Emphasize clean
water-ripple physics — circular ring, soft fade-out, no splashing yet.
Body remains leaning forward and steady; only the trunk and the ripple
animate. In the final ~0.5s, the trunk withdraws and the body straightens.
4 seconds.
```

**Veo:**
```
A static, locked-off camera with no camera movement of any kind. The baby
elephant's trunk taps down onto the surface of a small puddle three
times, in a slow, even, repeating rhythm — about one tap every second to
a second and a quarter. Each tap creates one small circular ripple on the
water that spreads outward and fades away before the next tap. The
character's body stays still and leaning forward; only the trunk and the
water ripple move. In the last moments of the shot, the trunk lifts away
and the character's body straightens back up. No splashing yet — just
gentle taps and ripples. 4 seconds.
```

## Scene 3 — Anticipation (0:06 – 0:08, 2s)

**Motion:** Almost no motion — a held-breath pause. Bubu is upright and completely still; only the smallest secondary motion (a slow blink, a faint breathing rise/fall) should occur, communicating stillness rather than animation. Puddle ripples from Scene 2 finish settling to perfectly calm within the first frames, then remain still. Trunk stays raised straight up, motionless.

**Higgsfield:**
```
Image-to-video from the Scene 3 keyframe. Motion strength: 1/10 (minimal —
near-freeze-frame). Bubu holds a completely still, upright pose — trunk
raised straight up, ears popped outward, eyes wide. Allow only the
smallest secondary motion: one slow blink partway through the clip and a
very subtle breathing rise/fall of the body. Any remaining ripple on the
puddle settles to perfectly flat and still within the first 0.5s, then
stays motionless for the rest of the shot. Camera: locked static, no
movement. Duration: 2s.
```

**Kling:**
```
Image-to-video, camera control: static (no camera motion).
Motion strength: very low — this shot should feel almost like a freeze-
frame. Subject: Bubu remains upright and still, trunk raised straight up,
ears held popped outward, eyes wide open. Only allow a single slow blink
and a faint breathing motion in the body — no other movement. The puddle's
last ripple fades to completely calm, flat water within the first half-
second, then stays still. Emphasize stillness and suspense. 2 seconds.
```

**Veo:**
```
A static, locked-off camera with zero camera movement. The baby elephant
character stands completely still in an upright "held breath" pose —
trunk raised straight up, ears popped out to the sides, eyes wide open.
The only motion allowed is a single slow eye blink and a very subtle
breathing movement of the body. The small puddle's surface finishes
settling into perfectly calm, still water within the first half-second
and then does not move again. The shot should feel like a tense, quiet
pause. 2 seconds.
```

## Scene 4 — Payoff (0:08 – 0:11, 3s)

**Motion:** Bubu's raised trunk swings downward and strikes the puddle. At the moment of impact, the puddle's color shifts from pale blue-gray to fully saturated turquoise and a burst of 2-3 water droplets splashes upward, then the droplets fall and settle. Simultaneously, Bubu's body shifts into the Delight pose — leaning back, one front foot lifting in a small hop, ears flapping outward, trunk swinging up and back into its raised hook position near the head.

**Higgsfield:**
```
Image-to-video from the Scene 4 keyframe. Motion strength: 6/10 (the
highest-energy beat of the episode, but keep it toddler-readable, not
chaotic). Bubu's trunk swings down quickly and strikes the puddle in the
first ~0.5s. At the moment of impact, the puddle's color rapidly shifts
from pale blue-gray to bright saturated turquoise, and 2-3 small water
droplets burst upward from the impact point. In the same motion, Bubu's
body leans backward, one front foot lifts off the ground in a small hop,
both ears flap outward, and the trunk swings up and back into a raised
hook position near the head. Over the remaining ~2.5s, the droplets arc
upward then fall and settle, and Bubu's body eases slightly toward a
resting stance. Camera: locked static, no movement, no zoom-in on impact.
Duration: 3s.
```

**Kling:**
```
Image-to-video, camera control: static (no camera motion).
Motion strength: medium-high, concentrated in the first second. Subject:
Bubu's trunk swings downward and taps the puddle; on contact, the puddle
rapidly changes from pale desaturated blue-gray to vivid saturated
turquoise, with 2-3 droplet shapes splashing upward in a clean arc.
Simultaneously, Bubu leans back, lifts one front foot in a small hop,
flaps both ears outward, and raises the trunk up and back near the head
in a celebratory pose. After the splash peak (~1s in), droplets fall and
settle and Bubu's pose eases slightly. Prioritize clean, readable splash/
droplet physics — no chaotic spray, no extra splashes. 3 seconds.
```

**Veo:**
```
A static, locked-off camera with absolutely no camera movement, pans, or
zooms — even on the impact. The baby elephant's trunk swings downward and
strikes the surface of the small puddle. At the instant of contact, the
puddle's water rapidly changes color from a pale grayish blue to a bright,
fully saturated turquoise, and 2 to 3 small droplets of water splash
upward and outward from the point of impact in a gentle arc. At the same
time, the character leans its body backward, lifts one front foot off the
ground in a small celebratory hop, flaps both ears outward, and swings its
trunk up and back near its head in a happy pose. Over the rest of the
shot, the droplets fall back down and the splash settles, and the
character's pose relaxes slightly. Keep the splash clean and readable, not
chaotic. 3 seconds.
```

## Scene 5 — Loop (0:11 – 0:12, 1s)

**Motion:** Bubu eases from the tail end of the Delight pose back into the Scene 1 resting "Curious" pose — body leveling out, foot returning to the ground, ears relaxing forward, trunk lowering into its resting curl. The puddle finishes fading from turquoise back to pale blue-gray. The final frame should closely match Scene 1's opening frame so the loop reads as continuous.

> **Generation note:** 1 second is shorter than the minimum clip length most video models support (typically 2s minimum). Generate this scene at the tool's minimum duration (commonly 2s) using the motion described below, then **trim to the final ~1s** (the portion where Bubu is closest to the Scene 1 resting pose and the puddle is calmest) during editing. See `editing_plan.md` for the trim approach.

**Higgsfield:**
```
Image-to-video from the Scene 4 end frame (or the Scene 5 keyframe).
Motion strength: 3/10. Bubu's pose eases out of the Delight hop and
settles back into a calm, level standing pose — foot returns to the
ground, ears relax forward, trunk lowers into a relaxed curl near the
mouth, eyes soften from crescents to the gentle "Curious" expression. The
puddle's color fades from turquoise back to pale blue-gray, and any
remaining droplets settle and disappear. Camera: locked static, no
movement. End on a calm pose closely matching the Scene 1 keyframe.
Generate at minimum supported duration; the final clip will be trimmed
to ~1s.
```

**Kling:**
```
Image-to-video, camera control: static (no camera motion).
Motion strength: low. Subject: Bubu transitions out of its excited
"Delight" pose into a calm, level "Curious" resting pose — lowered foot,
relaxed forward ears, trunk settling into a gentle curl near the mouth,
soft smile. The puddle gradually fades from saturated turquoise back to a
calm pale blue-gray with no droplets remaining. End frame should closely
resemble the Scene 1 starting frame. Generate at the minimum allowed
duration; will be trimmed to ~1s in editing.
```

**Veo:**
```
A static, locked-off camera with no camera movement. The baby elephant
character settles out of its excited, hopping pose into a calm, relaxed
standing pose — its lifted foot returns to the ground, its ears relax
forward, and its trunk lowers into a gentle resting curl near its mouth,
with a soft, content expression. The small puddle's color gradually fades
from bright turquoise back to a calm, pale grayish blue, and any water
droplets settle and disappear. The final frame should look very similar to
a calm resting pose of the character facing a calm pale puddle. Generate
at the shortest duration the model allows; this clip will be trimmed down
to about 1 second in editing.
```

## Cross-Scene Consistency Notes

- Always pass the **same approved Bubu reference image** as the identity/character reference alongside each scene's starting keyframe, where the tool supports a separate character-lock input (in addition to image-to-video's starting frame).
- Keep all "motion strength"/intensity values low except Scene 4 — toddlers track slow, readable motion better, and excessive motion strength risks distorting Bubu's proportions frame-to-frame.
- If any tool drifts Bubu's proportions, palette, or trunk shape during motion, regenerate using a lower motion-strength value before adjusting the prompt wording.
- None of these prompts should introduce on-screen text, sound, or new background elements — those are handled in `editing_plan.md` and at the image-prompt stage.
