# Episode 01 — "Bubu's Splash" — Scene Breakdown

**Pillar:** Water Play | **Hero color:** Turquoise `#4FD1C5` | **Build-beat word:** Tap | **Payoff word:** Splash | **Runtime:** 12s | **Audience:** 0-2 years

Reference: Bubu as shown in the approved reference sheet (soft-shaded toon baby elephant — pale gray-blue body, cream belly patch, large round ears with pink interior, short trunk with a curled hook tip, small rounded tail, blush cheeks).

---

## Scene 1 — Hello (0:00 – 0:02, 2s)

- **Visual description:** Bubu stands in a soft pastel meadow, facing toward a small, calm, pale blue-gray puddle in the lower-right third of the frame. Gentle idle sway.
- **Bubu expression:** Curious — soft raised eyebrows, gentle closed-curve smile, trunk hanging in a relaxed curl near the mouth, ears relaxed and forward.
- **Camera angle:** Static, locked-off, 9:16. Eye-level/slightly-below. Bubu occupies ~45% of frame height, positioned center-left, eyes at ~40% from top.
- **Object positions:** Pale blue-gray puddle, low saturation (~0.25), in the object-zone (right third), roughly ground-level.
- **On-screen text:** "Bubu!" (fades in, center-upper negative space).
- **Narration:** "Bubu!" (warm, single word, matches text).
- **Learning objective:** Character introduction; establishes the scene and invites the viewer to notice the puddle (visual curiosity hook).
- **Transition to next scene:** No cut. Bubu's body begins leaning forward and the trunk begins lifting toward the puddle as the clip continues — seamless motion into Scene 2.

---

## Scene 2 — Build (0:02 – 0:06, 4s)

- **Visual description:** Bubu leans forward and taps the puddle surface with the curled tip of its trunk, three times in a steady rhythm. Each tap produces one small ripple ring on the puddle.
- **Bubu expression:** Focused — one eyebrow very slightly lowered, eyes steady, ears still and forward, trunk extended forward and tapping downward toward the puddle on each beat.
- **Camera angle:** Same static 9:16 frame as Scene 1 — no cut, no camera movement.
- **Object positions:** Puddle remains pale blue-gray (~0.25 saturation); one small ripple ring appears and fades with each of the three taps.
- **On-screen text:** "Tap!" pulses in sync with each of the 3 taps (appears/grows slightly/fades, 3 times).
- **Narration:** "Tap! Tap! Tap!" — one word per tap, matching the on-screen pulses and the music's percussion ticks.
- **Learning objective:** Action vocabulary ("Tap") + early cause-and-effect (tap → ripple) + rhythm/repetition recognition.
- **Transition to next scene:** After the third tap, Bubu pulls the trunk back and the body straightens — no cut, direct continuation into the held-still Anticipation pose.

---

## Scene 3 — Anticipation (0:06 – 0:08, 2s)

- **Visual description:** Bubu pulls upright, goes completely still — a "held-breath" pause. The puddle's ripples settle to perfectly calm.
- **Bubu expression:** Surprise — both eyebrows raised high, eyes wide open, both ears popped outward revealing pink interior, mouth in a small open "o", trunk lifted straight up.
- **Camera angle:** Same static 9:16 frame — no cut, no camera movement.
- **Object positions:** Puddle calm, pale blue-gray, no ripples — the visual "quiet before the splash."
- **On-screen text:** None (per the rule that text appears only in Beats 1, 2, and 4).
- **Narration:** None — a brief held silence (no spoken word) to maximize the contrast with the payoff.
- **Learning objective:** Anticipation/suspense; non-verbal emotional-state recognition (wide eyes + popped ears = "something is about to happen").
- **Transition to next scene:** No cut. Bubu's raised trunk begins swinging down toward the puddle — the motion that triggers Scene 4 starts within this clip's final frames.

---

## Scene 4 — Payoff (0:08 – 0:11, 3s)

- **Visual description:** Bubu's trunk swings down and strikes the puddle. A burst of fully-saturated turquoise water splashes upward with a few droplet shapes, then settles. The puddle is now solid turquoise.
- **Bubu expression:** Delight — eyes scrunched into happy crescents, mouth in a wide open closed-curve smile, both ears flapped outward (pink visible), trunk raised in its rotated "hook" position (up and back toward the near ear), one front foot lifted in a small hop.
- **Camera angle:** Same static 9:16 frame — no cut, no camera movement, no zoom.
- **Object positions:** Puddle/splash shifts from pale blue-gray (~0.25 sat) to fully saturated turquoise `#4FD1C5` (~0.85 sat), with 2-3 small droplet shapes around it at peak saturation.
- **On-screen text:** "Splash!" — large, appears at the moment of impact, held through the settle.
- **Narration:** "Splash!" (bright, exclamatory, timed to the impact).
- **Learning objective:** Payoff word "Splash!"; color recognition (turquoise appears at peak saturation exactly when the word appears); single rewarding cause-and-effect resolution (Tap x3 → Splash).
- **Transition to next scene:** No cut. As droplets settle, Bubu's body eases back toward its resting pose and the puddle color begins fading back toward pale blue-gray — continuous motion into Scene 5.

---

## Scene 5 — Loop (0:11 – 0:12, 1s)

- **Visual description:** Bubu returns to its Scene 1 resting pose and framing. The puddle finishes fading back to pale blue-gray, ending on a frame that closely matches Scene 1's opening frame.
- **Bubu expression:** Curious — same as Scene 1 (soft smile, relaxed ears, trunk in resting curl, gentle sway).
- **Camera angle:** Same static 9:16 frame — no cut, no camera movement.
- **Object positions:** Puddle returns to pale blue-gray (~0.25 sat), matching Scene 1's starting state.
- **On-screen text:** None ("Tap!" and "Splash!" have already disappeared by this point; "Bubu!" does not reappear).
- **Narration:** None (or an optional very soft contented exhale/hum — no words).
- **Learning objective:** Reinforces the full cycle (curiosity → action → anticipation → result) and resets the scene for an immediate, satisfying replay.
- **Transition to next scene:** Hard cut back to Scene 1 (no transition effect) — the matching end/start frames make the loop read as continuous.
