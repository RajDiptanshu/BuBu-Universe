## 3. Video Prompts

Static, locked-off 9:16 camera throughout — no pans, tilts, zooms, or cuts. Image-to-video from each scene's keyframe; motion only.

1. **Hello (2s):** Gentle idle Wobble; corner pebble glows with a slow, soft steady pulse. In the final frames, Bubu's body begins leaning forward and the trunk begins lowering toward the bowl.
2. **Build (4s):** The trunk gently pats the bowl's water surface three times, evenly spaced (~1.3s each), each pat producing a small ripple that settles before the next — identical in speed and size. The corner pebble remains a steady soft glow. After the third pat, the trunk lifts and the body straightens.
3. **Anticipation (2s):** Near-freeze. The bowl's water settles to perfectly flat within the first 0.5s, then stays still and pale. Bubu holds upright, trunk straight up, ears popped outward; only a slow blink and faint breathing motion.
4. **Payoff (3s):** In the first ~0.5s the bowl's water rapidly brightens from pale gray-blue to fully saturated mint, with a few sparkle flecks rising off the surface; the corner pebble's glow brightens in the same beat. Simultaneously Bubu hops once, ears flap outward, trunk swings up and back. Over the remaining ~2.5s the sparkles fade and everything settles.
5. **Loop (1s, generated at 2s minimum then trimmed):** Bubu eases from the Delight hop back into its calm Scene-1 resting pose and trunk curl; the bowl's water settles to a soft steady mint glow and the corner pebble returns to its steady warm-cream glow. End frame should closely match Scene 1's opening frame except for the bowl's new glow.

## 5. Educational Objective

Introduces the routine word "Wash" and the result word "Clean," modeling the second step of the Daily Wonder Cycle ("Wash up in The Nook") and reinforcing that a calm, repeated gentle action (3 pats) leads to a satisfying, predictable result.

## 6. Brand-Building Objective

Reinforces The Nook as Bubu's recurring home base by giving it a second daily-routine object (the wash-bowl) alongside Episode 02's light-gap, while the Episode-02 corner pebble's brightening cameo shows viewers that *all* of The Nook's Wonder Pebbles persist and respond together — deepening the "the meadow remembers" mechanic (World Rule #8) within a single location.
