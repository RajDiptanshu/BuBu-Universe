## 3. Video Prompts

Static, locked-off 9:16 camera throughout — no pans, tilts, zooms, or cuts. Image-to-video from each scene's keyframe; motion only.

1. **Hello (2s):** Gentle idle Wobble; edge pebble glows with a slow, soft steady pulse. In the final frames, Bubu's body begins leaning forward and the trunk begins lowering toward the puddle.
2. **Build (4s):** The trunk dips into the puddle and flings an arc of droplets upward three times, evenly spaced (~1.3s each); each arc is visibly larger than the previous one (small → medium → large), each arcing and landing before the next dip. After the third arc, the trunk lifts and the body straightens, coiling slightly as if gathering energy.
3. **Anticipation (2s):** Near-freeze. The last droplets land and the puddle settles to perfectly flat water within the first 0.5s, then stays still. Bubu holds upright, trunk straight up, ears popped outward; only a slow blink and faint breathing motion — held a beat longer than usual to heighten anticipation.
4. **Payoff (3s):** In the first ~0.5s the trunk swings down and strikes the puddle in the biggest impact of the series; the puddle rapidly shifts from pale blue-gray to fully saturated turquoise with a large fan of 4–5 droplets bursting upward, and the edge pebble flares to its brightest glow yet in the same beat. Simultaneously Bubu performs its highest hop yet, ears flap outward, trunk swings up and back. Over the remaining ~2.5s droplets settle and the scene calms, the pebble's glow settling slightly warmer/brighter than before.
5. **Loop (1s, generated at 2s minimum then trimmed):** Bubu eases from the Delight hop back into its calm Scene-1 resting pose and trunk curl; the puddle fades from turquoise back to pale blue-gray and the edge pebble settles to a steady glow noticeably warmer/brighter than its Scene 1 starting level. End frame should closely match Scene 1's opening frame except for the pebble's elevated glow.

## 5. Educational Objective

Reinforces "good things take three tries" (World Rule #3) at its biggest scale yet — each Spray arc is bigger than the last — while pairing the build word "Fill" with the payoff word "Splash" to model anticipation-and-release as a complete, satisfying cycle that toddlers can predict and enjoy repeatedly.

## 6. Brand-Building Objective

Serves as the first-10-episode capstone: reinforces "Arre, Bubu! ...Splash!" as Bubu's signature catchphrase-and-payoff pairing at its most prominent treatment yet, introduces the "Spray" trunk function to round out the trunk-function vocabulary (Point, Tap/Pat, Hold, Spray), and shows the Episode-01 Wonder Pebble at its brightest glow to date — giving repeat viewers a clear, cumulative sense that Bubu's world remembers and grows with every episode.
