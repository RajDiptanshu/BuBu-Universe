## 3. Video Prompts

Static, locked-off 9:16 camera throughout — no pans, tilts, zooms, or cuts. Image-to-video from each scene's keyframe; motion only.

1. **Hello (2s):** Gentle idle Wobble; edge pebble glows with a slow, soft steady pulse. In the final frames, Bubu's body begins leaning forward and the trunk begins lifting toward the puddle.
2. **Build (4s):** The trunk taps the puddle three times, evenly spaced (~1.3s each); each resulting ripple ring is visibly larger than the previous one (small → medium → large), each expanding and fading before the next tap. After the third tap, the trunk lifts and the body straightens.
3. **Anticipation (2s):** Near-freeze. The largest ripple settles to perfectly flat water within the first 0.5s, then stays still. Bubu holds upright, trunk straight up, ears popped outward; only a slow blink and faint breathing motion.
4. **Payoff (3s):** In the first ~0.5s the trunk swings down and strikes the puddle; the puddle rapidly shifts from pale blue-gray to saturated turquoise with 2–3 droplets bursting upward, and the edge pebble's glow brightens in the same beat. Simultaneously Bubu hops once, ears flap outward, trunk swings up and back. Over the remaining ~2.5s droplets settle and the scene calms.
5. **Loop (1s, generated at 2s minimum then trimmed):** Bubu eases from the Delight hop back into its calm Scene-1 resting pose and trunk curl; the puddle fades from turquoise back to pale blue-gray and the edge pebble settles back to its steady soft glow. End frame should closely match Scene 1's opening frame.

## 5. Educational Objective

Deepens the cause-and-effect lesson from Episode 01 by making "good things take three tries" (World Rule #3) visually legible — each tap's ripple is bigger than the last, so a toddler can see the build toward the payoff, not just feel it.

## 6. Brand-Building Objective

Reinforces Bubu's signature payoff (trunk-tap → splash) in its home location while demonstrating, for the first time, that a Wonder Pebble created in an earlier episode persists and reacts in a later one — making Pebble Hill's "memory" mechanic feel alive even in a non-Pebble-Hill episode.
