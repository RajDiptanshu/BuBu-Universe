# Bubu Quality Checklist — Final Publish Gate

**Purpose:** This is the **last gate** before any Bubu episode is published, on any platform, in any language. It exists to catch four kinds of drift before they reach an audience:

1. **Character drift** — Bubu no longer looks/moves like Bubu (`bubu_core_identity_block.md`).
2. **Brand drift** — the episode no longer behaves like a Bubu episode (`bubu_personality_bible.md`, `bubu_franchise_vision.md`).
3. **Educational drift** — the episode no longer teaches one clear thing, clearly (`content_pillars.md`, `engagement_framework.md`).
4. **Visual drift** — the episode is not production-clean (`visual_style_guide.md`).

This checklist does not replace `bubu_consistency_checklist.md` (which scores individual reference/keyframe images on a 35-point scale during production). This document scores the **finished, edited, ready-to-publish episode as a whole**, and is the only gate that produces a publish/no-publish decision.

---

## Scoring System

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| **Maximum score** | 100 points |
| **Pass** | 90 – 100 |
| **Fail** | Below 90 |
| **Automatic Fail** | Any condition in Section "Automatic Fail Conditions" is present, **regardless of total score** |

A score of 90+ with zero automatic-fail conditions is required to move to **Episode Sign-Off**. There is no "publish with notes" tier — an episode either passes or is sent back for rework.

---

## A. Character Consistency — 40 points

Checked against `bubu_core_identity_block.md` and `bubu_consistency_checklist.md`. This is the heaviest-weighted section because **character consistency is the entire product** — a 1000-episode franchise lives or dies on Bubu looking like Bubu in episode 1 and episode 1000.

| Sub-category | Points | What "full marks" looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Identity & Proportions** | 6 | Single rounded teardrop body, no visible neck, two-heads-tall baby proportions (head ≈ 50% of height) hold across all 5 scenes and all keyframes. |
| **2. Silhouette** | 6 | Squint/silhouette test passes in every scene — ear circles, hook trunk, and teardrop body are each independently identifiable; the `#6B7C8C` outline is present, thin, and even around the full silhouette in every frame. |
| **3. Color Palette** | 8 | Body `#A9B7C4`, belly/face patch `#EAEFF2`, inner ears `#F4B8C0` (Surprise/Delight only), cheek blush `#FBC7C7`, eyes `#2B2B2B` with white highlight, outline `#6B7C8C` — all match within tolerance in every scene, with no drift toward brown/gray/green body tones. |
| **4. Ear Shape** | 6 | Both ears are perfect circles (~40% of head width, "two coins"), identical size, symmetric placement. Pink inner-ear color hidden in Curious/Focused, visible in Surprise/Delight, on **both** ears simultaneously. |
| **5. Trunk Shape & Rotation** | 8 | The fixed "hook" — uniform width, rounded tip, never tapering, never longer than one ear's diameter, always one smooth curve — and its rotation matches the pose table exactly: 0° (Curious), ~90° (Focused), ~180° (Surprise), ~215–225° (Delight). No closed loops, no second bends. |
| **6. Tail Shape** | 2 | Small rounded tail present and consistent in size/shape/position across all scenes; never absent, never elongated. |
| **7. Expression Correctness** | 4 | Each beat's expression matches its 4 signal channels (eyes/eyebrows, ears, trunk rotation, body posture) per the pose table — no ambiguity between Surprise and Delight. |

**Section A scoring rule:** Deduct points proportionally for *any* scene where a sub-category fails, even if other scenes are correct — one bad keyframe in a 5-scene episode is enough to lose points, because that one frame will be seen by the audience exactly as often as the other four.

## B. Personality Consistency — 20 points

Checked against `bubu_personality_bible.md`'s 8 signatures and `bubu_universe_bible.md` §8 (what children should feel).

| Sub-category | Points | What "full marks" looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Joyful Wonder present** | 4 | The emotional arc across the episode is Wonder → Focus → (brief) Anticipation → Joy → Wonder. The Payoff beat pairs Delight with "Arre, Bubu! ...[payoff word]!" — never delivered early, never delivered without the catchphrase. |
| **2. Bubu Wobble present** | 4 | Gentle idle sway runs continuously in Scenes 1, 2, and 5, and is **absent** in Scene 3 (Anticipation) — the absence itself must be clearly readable, not just "less motion." |
| **3. Ear-Perk & Point present** | 4 | Both ears lift/rotate ~10–15° forward at the *exact* instant the trunk snaps from its resting curl into the Build action, at the Scene 1→2 transition — and only there, once per episode. |
| **4. Curiosity present** | 3 | Scenes 1 and 5 show the Curious expression (soft raised eyebrows, relaxed forward ears, hook at 0°, small closed-curve smile) — the episode visibly "begins and ends in wonder," supporting the Loop. |
| **5. Fear level appropriate** | 3 | In standard-pillar episodes, no fear pose appears at all. In a Big Feelings/Whisper Grass episode, the fear pose (ears folded back, trunk tucked, Wobble stopped) is brief, never the final emotional note, and fully resolves to calm before any Delight payoff. |
| **6. No out-of-character behaviour** | 2 | No anger, no sadness-as-a-destination, no conflict, no aggression, no expression or motion outside Bubu's 4-expression / fixed-motion vocabulary. |

---

## C. Universe Consistency — 10 points

Checked against `bubu_universe_bible.md` (Locations, Wonder Pebbles, 8 World Rules).

| Sub-category | Points | What "full marks" looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Correct location** | 4 | The episode's location matches its assigned slot in `season_01_master_roadmap.md`, uses that location's established visual design (max 2 background set-pieces, correct base shapes), and does not introduce a new, undesigned location. |
| **2. Correct Wonder Pebble logic** | 4 | The episode either creates exactly one new Wonder Pebble or interacts with an existing one (per Season Guardrail #5), the pebble's color/glow state is consistent with its established history, and the Loop beat shows the correct persisted state. |
| **3. No violation of World Rules** | 2 | All 8 World Rules hold: one quiet world / one loud payoff; trunk-initiated wonder only; build = 1–3 tries; silent Anticipation beat; nothing stays scary; one sound per episode; round shapes only; the meadow remembers (no permanent change except Pebble Hill). |

---

## D. Educational Consistency — 10 points

Checked against `content_pillars.md` and `engagement_framework.md`.

| Sub-category | Points | What "full marks" looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Single learning objective** | 3 | The episode teaches exactly one concept (one pillar, one vocabulary pair) — no mixed lessons, no "bonus" teaching crammed into the same 12 seconds. |
| **2. Age appropriate** | 2 | Pacing, complexity, and emotional range stay within the 0–2 (primary) / 2–4 (secondary) target — nothing requires reading, counting beyond 3, or multi-step reasoning. |
| **3. Clear vocabulary target** | 3 | The Build word and Payoff word are each 1–2 words, large, high-contrast, placed in the reserved negative space, and appear only at the correct beats (Build word pulses ×2–3 in Scene 2; payoff word appears once, at the instant of Payoff). |
| **4. Clear payoff** | 2 | The Payoff is a single, unambiguous, fully-saturated (0.75–0.90) moment — one object, one color, one sound — with no competing focal points. |

---

## E. Production Quality — 10 points

Checked against `visual_style_guide.md` and general AI-generation hygiene.

| Sub-category | Points | What "full marks" looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **1. No AI artifacts** | 3 | No extra limbs, merged shapes, melting geometry, duplicated objects, or warped backgrounds in any frame. |
| **2. No broken anatomy** | 3 | Four legs, two ears, one trunk, one tail, two eyes — every frame, every scene, no missing or duplicated features, no impossible poses. |
| **3. No visual glitches** | 2 | No flicker, color banding, palette pop, or background inconsistency between adjacent frames/scenes; Scene 5's final frame closely matches Scene 1's opening frame for a clean loop. |
| **4. No camera errors** | 2 | Static, locked-off 9:16 camera throughout — no pans, tilts, zooms, drifts, or cuts; Bubu remains at 40–55% of frame height, eyes ~40% from top, center-left. |

---

## F. Franchise Building — 10 points

Checked against `bubu_franchise_vision.md` and the cumulative continuity established by prior episodes.

| Sub-category | Points | What "full marks" looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Strengthens Bubu recognition** | 4 | The Bubu Wobble, Ear-Perk & Point, and "Arre, Bubu!" all appear correctly and in their canonical positions — a repeat viewer recognizes "this is a Bubu episode" within the first 2 seconds. |
| **2. Strengthens recurring world** | 3 | The episode reinforces an established location and/or advances "the meadow remembers" (Pebble Hill growth, persisted glow-objects) without contradicting prior episodes. |
| **3. Strengthens future merchandising potential** | 3 | Every new visual element (objects, pebbles, props) is simple, round, flat-color, and silhouette-clean — derivable into a plush, sticker, or board-book illustration with zero redesign. |

# 1. Red Flag List

These are **not automatic fails**, but any one of them should trigger a closer second-pass review before scoring, and a pattern of red flags across multiple episodes is itself a signal of compounding drift (see `bubu_consistency_checklist.md`'s periodic drift check).

- Trunk rotation is "close enough" between two adjacent poses (e.g., Surprise vs. Delight read ambiguously).
- One ear's pink interior is visible but the other's isn't (or vice versa) during Surprise/Delight.
- Background set-piece count is at the 2-piece limit *and* looks visually busy.
- The Build beat's repetitions are not perfectly identical in timing/size (unless the episode deliberately uses the "grows each time" variant, per World Rule #3).
- A Wonder Pebble's glow intensity doesn't visibly differ between "newly lit" and "established" states.
- On-screen text sits close to — but not clearly inside — the reserved negative space.
- The single SFX is slightly early or late relative to the Payoff visual (more than ~2 frames of drift).
- Music swell peaks before or after the Payoff moment rather than exactly on it.
- The Loop beat's final frame is "close" to Scene 1 but a careful side-by-side reveals a mismatch (pose, framing, or background detail).
- An episode's hero color is a near-duplicate of another recently-published episode's hero color (risk of color-identity confusion across the catalog).
- Narration pacing feels rushed or compressed compared to prior episodes in the same pillar.

# 2. Automatic Fail Conditions

If **any** of the following are present, the episode is an automatic fail regardless of total score. These map to `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §9 ("What Bubu Should Never Become") and the highest-risk failure modes in `bubu_master_prompt.md`.

- **Tusks, wrinkles, skin texture, or toenails** appear anywhere, in any frame.
- **A second character** (parent elephant, animal friend, or any other figure) appears anywhere outside an approved long-form/Friendship Pond context.
- **The trunk** is rendered as a long, tapering, segmented, hose-like, or closed-loop shape in any frame.
- **The ears** are rendered as floppy, oval, leaf-shaped, or mismatched in size in any frame.
- **The fixed color palette** is broken — body color drifts to brown, flat neutral gray, or green; or the `#6B7C8C` outline is missing entirely.
- **More than one sound effect** plays, or the single SFX plays anywhere other than the Payoff moment.
- **More than 1–2 words** of on-screen text appear at once, or any dialogue/song lyrics appear.
- **Hindi songs or dialogue-heavy storytelling** appear in a standard-pillar episode (reserved exclusively for the Hindi-expansion process in `channel_launch_plan.md`).
- **The episode ends on fear, sadness, anger, or any unresolved negative emotion.**
- **The camera pans, tilts, zooms, or cuts** at any point.
- **A Wonder Pebble's established state is contradicted** (e.g., a previously-lit pebble appears unlit with no explanation, or two episodes claim the same pebble as "new").
- **The episode mixes more than one pillar's learning objective** in a way that obscures the single vocabulary target.
- **Runtime falls outside the 10–15 second range**, or the Loop beat does not return to Scene 1's framing.

# 3. Review Process

Bubu's review process is deliberately small and sequential — designed to scale from a 1-person operation to a small studio team without changing structure, only headcount.

**Step 1 — Self-Review (Creator/Editor).**
The person who assembled the episode scores it against this entire checklist *before* requesting any other review. Any score below 90, or any automatic-fail condition, goes back to rework immediately — it does not proceed to Step 2.

**Step 2 — Canon Cross-Check (Second Reviewer, or AI assistant acting as second reviewer).**
A second pass, ideally by a different person (or a fresh AI session with no memory of the production decisions made), focused specifically on:
- Section A (Character Consistency) and Section C (Universe Consistency) — the two sections most likely to drift silently because the creator has "gotten used to" a small deviation.
- The Red Flag List — explicitly check each item.
- The Automatic Fail Conditions — explicitly check each item.

The second reviewer scores independently, without seeing the first reviewer's score, then the two scores are compared. A gap of more than 5 points in any section triggers a discussion before either score is finalized.

**Step 3 — Continuity Check (against prior episodes).**
Using `season_01_master_roadmap.md` and the most recent 2–3 published episode packages, confirm:
- Location, hero color, and Wonder Pebble states are consistent with what's already been published.
- No hero color, build word, or payoff word is an unintentional near-duplicate of a very recent episode.
- The episode's position in the season arc (per the roadmap's Arc Overview) is honored.

**Step 4 — Final Score Reconciliation.**
The two reviewers' scores are reconciled into one final score and one final pass/fail decision. If the final score is 90+ with zero automatic-fail conditions, the episode proceeds to Sign-Off. Otherwise, it returns to the relevant production stage (per `production_bible.md`'s pipeline) with specific, itemized notes — never a vague "needs work."

---

# 4. Episode Sign-Off Process

Sign-off is the final, recorded act that converts a "finished episode" into a "publishable asset." Every signed-off episode should leave a permanent record, the same way a Pixar shot is "approved" before going to final render, or a Bluey episode is "locked" before broadcast delivery.

**Sign-off requires:**

1. **Final score sheet** — the completed A–F scoring table (this document), with the final reconciled score and a clear PASS (≥90) marked.
2. **Zero automatic-fail conditions**, explicitly confirmed (a simple "none present" line, checked by both reviewers).
3. **Episode metadata confirmed** — episode number, pillar, location, hero color, build/payoff words, and Wonder Pebble delta (created vs. interacted-with), matching `season_01_master_roadmap.md`.
4. **Export confirmation** — 9:16 vertical, ≥1080×1920, MP4 (H.264), 30fps, correct filename per `production_checklist.md` Phase 7 convention.
5. **Metadata package attached** — title, description, hashtags, and thumbnail, per `channel_launch_plan.md`'s metadata strategy.

**Sign-off record format** (append one row per episode to the running episode tracker):

| Episode # | Title | Final Score | Pass/Fail | Reviewer 1 | Reviewer 2 | Date | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | Bubu's Splash | 94 | PASS | — | — | — | — |

**After sign-off:**
- The episode moves to Publishing (per `production_bible.md` §2 and `channel_launch_plan.md`).
- The Wonder Pebble state established/changed by this episode is recorded as the new "current state" for the next episode's Continuity Check (Step 3 above).
- Any near-miss notes (red flags that didn't fail the episode but were close) are logged for the next periodic drift review, per `bubu_consistency_checklist.md`.

**No episode is published without a completed sign-off record.** This is the one process step with zero exceptions — it is the difference between "an episode that looks right to one person" and "an episode that is provably consistent with 1000 others."
