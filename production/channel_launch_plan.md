# Bubu Channel Launch Plan

**Purpose:** This document defines how Bubu goes from "a complete canon and a library of episode packages" to "India's next preschool character brand." It is a **strategic** document — every tactical choice here (schedule, metadata, dashboards, triggers) exists to serve `bubu_franchise_vision.md`, and nothing here may be optimized in a way that contradicts it. Where this document gives a number (a date, a subscriber count, a percentage), treat it as a **planning target to calibrate against**, not a guarantee — the qualitative bar (calm, consistent, trusted) is the real success criterion, and the numbers exist to tell us whether the brand is being *built*, not just *watched*.

---

# 1. Launch Philosophy

## Why We Are Launching

Bubu is not launching to win a week of YouTube Shorts impressions. Bubu is launching to become a **recognizable character** — the kind of character a parent in Mumbai, Pune, or Delhi names without prompting when asked "what does your toddler watch?" Per `bubu_franchise_vision.md`, the channel is the **acquisition and proof-of-concept layer** for a much larger plan: Hindi-language expansion, long-form/bedtime content, plush toys, board books, and eventually a companion app. None of that is possible without a channel that (a) exists, (b) is consistent, and (c) earns trust over months, not days.

We are launching now because the canon (Identity → Personality → Universe → Content Pillars → Engagement Framework → Visual Style), the Season 1 roadmap, and a working episode-production pipeline all exist and are internally consistent (`production/quality_checklist.md` gives us a repeatable pass/fail gate). Launching is the point at which the brand starts accumulating the one thing it cannot get any other way: **a public history of consistency**.

## Success Criteria

Success is measured in **three layers**, in this priority order:

1. **Brand consistency at scale** — every published episode passes `production/quality_checklist.md` at 90+. This is non-negotiable and is checked *before* any metric below is considered. A viral episode that fails the quality gate is a **failure**, not a win — it teaches the algorithm (and the audience) to expect something Bubu cannot reliably deliver.
2. **Audience trust signals** — high average-percentage-viewed (ideally loop-driven, >100%), high returning-viewer share, and low first-2-second drop-off (the Bubu Wobble + Ear-Perk & Point should hold attention immediately). These are *leading* indicators of "parents trust this enough to press play without checking first."
3. **Growth metrics** — subscribers, views, CTR. These matter, but only as a *consequence* of layers 1 and 2. A launch plan that hits subscriber targets while failing quality gates or showing high drop-off has not succeeded — it has borrowed against the brand's future.

**The single sentence test for every launch decision:** *"Does this make Bubu more recognizable and more trusted six months from now, or does it just make this week's numbers look better?"* If a tactic only serves the second half of that question, it is rejected.

# 2. Inventory Before Launch

## Minimum Launch Inventory: 15 episodes

**Reasoning:**
- At a realistic post-launch cadence (Section 3: 3-4 uploads/week ramping to daily), 15 episodes covers **3-4 weeks** of content — enough runway to start observing real retention data, iterate on metadata (Section 4), and build the analytics dashboard (Section 5) before the pipeline needs to keep pace in real time.
- 15 episodes (Episodes 1-15 of the roadmap) covers three of the four content pillars used in Arc 1 (Water Play, Daily Routines, Counting, plus the first Colors and Shapes intros at Eps 13-14) — enough **pillar variety** to avoid the channel reading as repetitive in its first weeks, while staying entirely within Arc 1's "Bubu identity / discovery" focus.
- By Episode 15, the Wonder Pebble mechanic has been seeded (Ep 1, 3), reinforced (Ep 4, 5), and grown (Ep 8) — enough for **early repeat viewers** to notice "the Hill has more pebbles than last time," which is the first real test of the franchise's core continuity hook.
- 15 is the floor below which a launch risks looking like a "test channel" rather than an established one — preschool-content parents (and the algorithm) respond to channels that already look like they have a rhythm.

## Ideal Launch Inventory: 20 episodes (all of Arc 1)

**Reasoning:**
- 20 episodes = **the complete Arc 1**, ending with Episode 20 ("Bubu's First Day"), which explicitly names the Daily Wonder Cycle and serves as Arc 1's capstone. Launching with the full arc means the channel's first month of content has genuine narrative completeness — a new viewer who binges all 20 sees a *finished chapter*, not a work-in-progress.
- By Episode 20, all of the following are established and provable on-screen: the Bubu Wobble, Ear-Perk & Point, "Arre, Bubu!", all 4 trunk functions (Point, Hold, Spray, Pat/Tap — completed by Ep 18), the full core routine-verb set (wash/eat/brush/sleep/all-done, Eps 6/9/11/15/19), numbers 1-3 (Eps 5/8/12), first visits to Color Garden and Shape Hill (Eps 13-14), and a visibly-growing Pebble Hill (Eps 3/5/8/17).
- 20 episodes gives ~4 weeks of daily-equivalent content with **zero need to "catch up" mid-launch** — the production team can spend the first month focused on Section 5's dashboard and Section 3's schedule tuning, not racing to produce Episode 21 before Episode 20 runs out.
- **Recommendation:** treat 15 as the absolute floor to begin a soft/unlisted launch (testing metadata, thumbnails, upload mechanics privately), and 20 as the target for the **public launch announcement**. The gap between 15 and 20 (5 episodes, all within Arc 1) is the highest-priority production work between now and launch day.

# 3. Launch Schedule — First 30 Days

## Upload Frequency

| Days | Cadence | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Days 1-7 | **3 uploads** (Mon/Wed/Fri or similar spread) | A new channel has no watch-history signal yet. Spacing the first week's uploads lets each one get a fair individual impression window from the algorithm, and lets the team observe per-episode performance (Section 6) before committing to a daily cadence. |
| Days 8-21 | **5 uploads/week** (skip 2 non-consecutive days) | Once early retention data confirms the quality bar is holding (Section 1, layer 1-2), increase frequency. Five per week still leaves buffer days for QA re-shoots if an episode fails `quality_checklist.md`. |
| Days 22-30 | **Daily (7/week)** | By week 4, the channel should be at the **daily cadence** that `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §5 identifies as the long-term Shorts standard. Reaching daily by day ~22 means the channel has ~8 days of daily-cadence data before the 30-day mark, enough for a first read on whether daily is sustainable at current production capacity. |

**If the 20-episode "ideal inventory" (Section 2) is used:** at the cadence above, all 20 episodes are consumed by roughly day 25-28 — meaning production of Episodes 21+ should begin no later than **launch day itself**, not after.

## Recommended Posting Times (India, IST)

Two daily windows, chosen around when **parents** (the actual decision-makers, per `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §1) are most likely to be selecting content for a toddler:

| Window | Time (IST) | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | **7:30-8:30 PM** | Post-dinner, pre-bedtime wind-down window for urban Indian households with toddlers — exactly the "calm screen time" use case Bubu is built for. This is the primary posting slot. |
| **Secondary** | **8:00-9:30 AM** | Morning routine window (breakfast/getting-ready time) — a second natural "hand the phone to a toddler for a few calm minutes" moment. Useful for the days with multiple uploads (weeks 3-4). |

When only one upload per day is scheduled, **always use the evening (primary) window** — it aligns most directly with Bubu's "calm before bed" brand positioning and is less competitive than the crowded morning content slot.

# 4. Metadata Strategy

## Title Formula

```
Bubu [Build/Payoff Word]! [Emoji] | [Pillar/Theme] for Toddlers
```

- **`[Build/Payoff Word]`** — the episode's actual payoff word (e.g., "Splash," "Morning," "One"), always capitalized, always with an exclamation mark — mirrors the energy of "Arre, Bubu! ...Splash!"
- **`[Emoji]`** — exactly one emoji, matching the hero color/object (💦 for water, 🐾 for pebbles, ☀️ for morning, etc.) — never more than one, to avoid visual clutter in search/feed listings.
- **`[Pillar/Theme] for Toddlers`** — a short, parent-search-friendly phrase (e.g., "Water Play for Toddlers," "Counting for Toddlers," "Morning Routine for Toddlers") that signals educational value to the actual decision-maker.

**Examples (using existing episode packages):**

| Episode | Title |
|---|---|
| Ep 01 — Bubu's Splash | **Bubu Splash! 💦 \| Water Play for Toddlers** |
| Ep 02 — Good Morning, Bubu | **Bubu Morning! ☀️ \| Morning Routine for Toddlers** |
| Ep 05 — Bubu's Pebble Pat | **Bubu One! 🐾 \| Counting for Toddlers** |
| Ep 09 — Snack Time | **Bubu Yum! 🍑 \| Daily Routines for Toddlers** |

## Description Formula

```
[1 sentence naming the build word and payoff word] [1 sentence inviting replay, framed gently — never "like and subscribe" pressure language].

#Bubu #[PillarTag] #[KeywordTag] #ToddlerLearning #PreschoolLearning
```

- The first sentence should be readable as a tiny "what happens" — useful for parents scanning descriptions and for search/SEO.
- The second sentence should invite replay through *curiosity*, never urgency ("Watch Bubu pat the pebble until it glows turquoise — can you guess what happens each time?").
- Hashtags appear at the end of the description (see below), never inline.

**Examples:**

> **Ep 01 — Bubu's Splash:**
> Bubu taps the puddle three times, and — splash! — the water turns bright turquoise. Watch Bubu's happy hop every time the puddle lights up!
> #Bubu #WaterPlay #Splash #ToddlerLearning #PreschoolLearning

> **Ep 05 — Bubu's Pebble Pat:**
> Bubu gently pats one little pebble on Pebble Hill until it glows turquoise — "Arre, Bubu! ...One!" Can your little one count along with Bubu?
> #Bubu #Counting #PebbleHill #ToddlerLearning #PreschoolLearning

## Hashtag Strategy

A **3-tier hashtag system**, always totaling 5 tags (the practical legibility limit before tags look like spam):

| Tier | Count | Purpose | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Core brand tags** | 1 | Always present, every episode | `#Bubu` |
| **Pillar tag** | 1 | One of the 8 content pillars | `#WaterPlay`, `#Counting`, `#DailyRoutines`, `#Colors`, `#Shapes` |
| **Keyword/location tag** | 1 | Episode-specific — the build/payoff word or location | `#Splash`, `#PebbleHill`, `#Morning` |
| **Category tags** | 2 | Audience/format signal, reused across all episodes | `#ToddlerLearning`, `#PreschoolLearning` |

This structure is fixed precisely so it **never needs creative decisions per episode** beyond picking the pillar and keyword tags — removing friction from the publishing step and keeping the hashtag "fingerprint" of the channel recognizable.

## Playlist Strategy

- One **"Bubu — Start Here"** playlist containing Episodes 1-20 (Arc 1) in order — the recommended entry point for new viewers, framed as "Bubu's first 20 days."
- One playlist **per content pillar** (e.g., "Bubu — Water Play," "Bubu — Counting with Bubu," "Bubu — Daily Routines with Bubu") — supports parents searching for a specific learning focus.
- One **"Pebble Hill Story"** playlist containing only the World/Treasure episodes (3, 5, 8, 17, ...) in order — this is the channel's "lore" playlist, aimed at repeat viewers and (eventually) older siblings/parents who notice the continuity.

# 5. Analytics Dashboard

Tracked weekly for the first 90 days, then monthly. All targets below are **calibration targets for a new channel in the kids/preschool niche** — they are deliberately conservative; consistently beating them is good news, consistently missing them by a wide margin (not a little) is a signal to revisit Section 1's quality gate before touching metadata.

| Metric | What it tells us | Target (first 90 days) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CTR (Click-Through Rate)** | Whether the thumbnail + title earn the click | **4-6%** | Kids-content CTR is typically lower than general content because much viewing is parent-initiated/passive feed scrolling rather than active searching — 4-6% is healthy for this category. Below 3% sustained → revisit thumbnail consistency (Bubu's silhouette must read at 120px, per `bubu_consistency_checklist.md` §7). |
| **Average View Duration (AVD)** | Raw seconds watched | **≥10 seconds** (of a 12-second episode) | At 10+ seconds, a viewer has seen Build, Anticipation, and Payoff — the full "story." Below 8 seconds sustained suggests the Hello beat isn't holding attention (check Bubu Wobble/Ear-Perk visibility in the first frame). |
| **Average Percentage Viewed** | AVD as % of runtime, **can exceed 100% via loop replays** | **80-100%+** | This is Bubu's *signature metric* — because Scene 5's final frame matches Scene 1's opening frame (the Loop), a satisfied viewer often watches twice without realizing it. >100% average is a strong positive signal that the Loop mechanic is working as designed. |
| **Retention curve shape** | Where viewers drop off within the 12s | **Flat-to-rising curve**, no cliff before second 2 | A cliff in the first 2 seconds (the Hello beat) is the highest-priority retention problem — it means the Bubu Wobble / Ear-Perk & Point aren't registering fast enough. A gentle rise toward the Payoff (second 8) is the ideal shape. |
| **Subscriber growth rate** | Audience accumulation | **Week-over-week growth, even if small (5-15%/week early on)** | Absolute subscriber counts matter less in month 1 than *consistent* growth — a flat or declining trend for 2+ consecutive weeks (even at low absolute numbers) is the trigger for a Section 1 quality review. |
| **Returning viewer %** | Trust/habit formation — the most important long-term metric | **Target: steadily increasing from baseline**; by day 30, returning viewers should represent a **growing share** of total views | This is the metric most directly tied to `bubu_franchise_vision.md`'s "most trusted" positioning. A channel that only ever reaches new viewers (returning % flat or near-zero) is not yet a *brand* — it's a content firehose. Returning-viewer growth is the earliest quantitative evidence that Bubu is becoming a recognized character rather than "a video that played once." |

# 6. Episode Performance Framework

Every published episode is classified after it has accumulated at least 7 days of data. Classification drives action (Section 6's table below), not blame — a Failure Episode is a **data point about the format**, not a verdict on the people who made it.

| Classification | Definition (relative to channel's trailing 10-episode average) | Typical signals | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hit Episode** | Average % viewed **≥125% of channel average**, AND retention curve shows no early cliff, AND subscriber conversion rate above channel average | Strong loop replay behavior; often a Water Play or Counting episode (Section 7's easiest pillars to make instantly legible); frequently a Wonder Pebble milestone episode | **Study it.** Identify which specific element (pacing, payoff timing, hero color, on-screen text size) might be replicable, and note it for the next batch of episode packages. Do **not** mechanically "repeat the formula" — Bubu's consistency comes from the *canon*, not from chasing one viral pattern. |
| **Average Episode** | Average % viewed **between 80% and 125%** of channel average, retention curve roughly matches the channel's typical shape | The expected outcome for most episodes — Bubu is designed for *consistency*, not for every episode to be a standalone hit | **No action required.** This is the healthy baseline. A channel where most episodes are "Average" by this definition is performing exactly as designed. |
| **Failure Episode** | Average % viewed **below 60% of channel average**, OR a retention cliff in the first 2 seconds, OR the episode scored below 90 on `production/quality_checklist.md` despite being published (process failure) | Possible causes: a quality-gate miss that slipped through, a confusing or off-pattern thumbnail, a hero color too close to a recently-published episode (see Quality Checklist's Red Flag List), or a pillar/location combination that's genuinely harder to make legible at 12 seconds | **Two-step review:** (1) Re-run the episode against `production/quality_checklist.md` — if it now scores below 90, the process gap that allowed publishing is the priority fix. (2) If it scores 90+, the issue is likely metadata/thumbnail or an unusually difficult pillar/pacing combination — note it, but do not alter the canon based on a single data point. |

**Important guardrail:** Never let a single "Hit Episode" change the canon (`bubu_core_identity_block.md` through `visual_style_guide.md`). The canon is the asset that makes 1000 episodes possible; a viral outlier is, by definition, an outlier. Patterns become canon-relevant only after they hold across **multiple** Hit Episodes spanning different pillars.

# 7. Shorts Strategy

Shorts remain the channel's **core format and primary growth engine**, per `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §5. Organization principles:

- **Pillar rotation, not pillar blocks.** Do not publish 5 Water Play episodes in a row even if they're ready first — rotate across pillars within each week (per the Season 1 roadmap's natural ordering) so the channel's *feed* itself teaches "Bubu's world is varied but always calm," and so no single pillar's performance dominates the early data (Section 6).
- **Roadmap order is the default publishing order.** `season_01_master_roadmap.md`'s episode sequence already encodes dependency rules (e.g., Pebble Hill "grows" episodes require earlier pebble-creating episodes to have aired first). Do not reorder for "metadata reasons" — narrative dependency outranks short-term optimization.
- **Every episode is loop-bait by design.** Because Scene 5 mirrors Scene 1, every Short is implicitly an "infinite" video on platforms that auto-replay. No additional "watch again" prompts or end-cards are needed or wanted — they would violate the "never manipulative" rule (`bubu_franchise_vision.md` §9.4).
- **The "Pebble Hill Story" playlist (Section 4) is the retention engine for repeat viewers.** New viewers come in via individual Shorts (algorithm-driven); the World/Treasure episodes, watched in playlist order, are what convert a casual viewer into someone who notices "the Hill has more pebbles now" — the first real test of brand attachment beyond a single video.
- **No Shorts series should ever require watching a previous episode to "make sense."** Every episode must be a complete 12-second experience on its own (per the Engagement Framework) — continuity (Pebble Hill, Wonder Pebbles) is a *bonus layer* for repeat viewers, never a requirement for new ones.

# 8. Long-Form Strategy

Per `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §6, long-form is a **year 2+ expansion built from existing Shorts assets** — it is explicitly *not* a parallel production pipeline. The triggers below are about **when enough Shorts inventory and audience exist** to make a compilation valuable, not about starting new production work.

| Format | Trigger | Composition |
|---|---|---|
| **10-minute compilations** | Backlog of **≥30 published Shorts** AND subscriber base showing consistent week-over-week growth (Section 5) — roughly the point where Arc 1 + early Arc 2 are complete | ~8-10 Shorts strung together with the Intro/Outro bumpers (`bubu_personality_bible.md` §7-8) as bookends, grouped by pillar or by the "Pebble Hill Story" continuity thread |
| **30-minute compilations** | Backlog of **≥60 published Shorts** AND first comfort/fear episode (Ep 55, per the roadmap) has aired — only once the "always resolves" pattern (World Rule #5) has been demonstrated at least once, a longer-form "calm down" compilation can credibly use it | A curated mix favoring Daily Routines and Big Feelings/Whisper Grass episodes (per `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §6's "bedtime/wind-down compilations") — explicitly positioned as a sleep/wind-down aid |
| **1-hour compilations** | Season 1 substantially or fully complete (**≥80-100 episodes**) AND the channel has demonstrated sustained returning-viewer growth (Section 5) | Full-library "ambient Bubu" compilations — designed for background/passive viewing in the home, the long-form equivalent of "the channel a family puts on" rather than "a video someone chose" |

**Hard rule carried over from the vision document:** long-form is also the **only** context in which Animal Friends / Friendship Pond content may appear (Episodes 81-86 and the 91-92 tease in the roadmap are Shorts-format *teases* only — any actual side-character introduction happens in long-form, never inserted into the Shorts roadmap).

# 9. Hindi Expansion Strategy

This section is a direct execution plan for `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §3, sequenced against `season_01_master_roadmap.md` Arc 4 (Episodes 61-80).

## When Hindi Words Enter

**Episode 61 ("Pani, Splash!")** — the first Hindi word, "Pani" (water), is paired on-screen with the already-known English word "Water," inside a Water Play episode (Bubu's favorite-thing pillar, per `bubu_personality_bible.md` §4). This is not arbitrary: per the roadmap's Episode Dependency Notes, **no Hindi word may appear before its matching English concept has already been taught** — "Pani/Water" works at Episode 61 specifically because Water Play (and the word "Splash") has been reinforced repeatedly since Episode 1.

Each subsequent Arc 4 episode follows the same pairing rule (e.g., "Lal/Red" at Ep 65 depends on "A Red Hello" at Ep 13; "Ek/One" at Ep 62 depends on "Bubu's Pebble Pat" at Ep 5). **The English feed is never paused or replaced** — Hindi words are layered onto it, exactly as the vision document specifies.

## When Hindi Rhymes Enter

**Hindi rhymes/songs do not enter the core channel at any point covered by this plan.** This is a hard constraint carried over from the episode-creation guardrails ("Do not introduce Hindi songs") and from `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §9.1-9.2 (never loud/song-driven, never emotionally wide). If a dedicated Hindi-language feed (below) is launched, any music remains the same continuous instrumental bed used in English-feed episodes — Hindi enters through **on-screen text and narration vocabulary only**, never through rhyme or song structure.

## When Bilingual Episodes Begin

**Episode 80 ("Do Bhasha Din" / "Two-Language Day")** — the Arc 4 capstone — is the first **fully bilingual** episode: a complete Daily Wonder Cycle (Nook → Puddle → Pebble Hill) with bilingual on-screen text throughout. This is positioned as a **proof point**, not a permanent format change to the main feed — per the vision document, bilingual content "is explored for diaspora-targeted content, but never at the cost of the 1-2 word/large-text legibility rule." Episode 80's performance directly informs whether bilingual treatment expands.

## Trigger for a Dedicated Hindi-Language Feed

Per `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §3, the Hindi feed is **not a dub** — it's the same pipeline with a swapped language layer, gated on Arc 4 (Eps 61-80) proving that:
1. Hindi-word episodes (61, 62, 65, ...) show **retention at or above the channel's English-only average** (Section 5/6 framework) — i.e., a Hindi word does not depress engagement relative to an equivalent English-only episode.
2. Episode 80's bilingual treatment performs at or above "Average Episode" classification (Section 6).

If both hold, the Hindi feed becomes a candidate for Section 10's franchise expansion triggers.

# 10. Franchise Expansion Triggers

Each expansion below is gated on **both** a roadmap dependency (from `season_01_master_roadmap.md`) **and** a metric threshold (from Sections 5-6) — narrative readiness alone, or metric readiness alone, is not sufficient. This dual-gate approach is the direct implementation of `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §9.6 ("never expand the cast or world faster than the canon supports").

| Expansion | Roadmap Dependency | Metric Trigger | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Friendship Pond (tease)** | Episode 91 — only after all other 9 locations have each appeared at least once (per Episode Dependency Notes) | Channel shows sustained returning-viewer growth (Section 5) by the time Arc 5 begins | This is a **distant visual tease only** (a ripple, no character) — the tease itself requires no metric gate beyond "the channel is healthy enough that a long-time viewer would notice it." |
| **Recurring side characters (Animal Friends)** | Long-form has launched (Section 8) AND Friendship Pond has been teased (Ep 91-92) | First long-form compilation (10-minute tier) classifies as "Average" or better (Section 6) | Per the vision document, a second character's introduction "deserves more narrative space than a 12s loop allows" — this is the **first and only** point at which the locked single-character roster expands, and only in long-form. |
| **Dedicated Hindi-language channel/feed** | Arc 4 (Eps 61-80) complete | Both Section 9 metric conditions met (Hindi-word retention ≥ English baseline; Ep 80 ≥ "Average") | This is the first **proof point** for India-market investment per the vision document — treat it as the gateway to all India-specific expansions (merch, partnerships, OTT). |
| **Merchandise (plush toys first)** | Season 1 at least 50% complete (≥50 episodes) — enough vocabulary/visual variety to make a product line feel like "a world," not "a single video's character" | Subscriber count and brand-recognition signals (e.g., comments/searches referencing "Bubu," "Arre Bubu," or "the elephant who pats pebbles" without prompting) show organic, unprompted recognition | Plush toys are first per the vision document because Bubu's design requires **zero redesign** for plush construction — lowest-risk, highest-symbolism first product. |
| **Wonder Pebbles as physical collectibles** | Pebble Hill has visibly "grown" multiple times (Eps 17, 33, 59, ... per roadmap) | Follows directly after plush toy launch — no independent metric gate, sequenced as the second product | Directly extends the in-world mechanic the audience has already been watching accumulate for months. |
| **Board books (bilingual)** | At least one full arc of vocabulary pairs exists in both languages (post-Arc 4, Ep 80+) | Hindi feed (above) has launched | Maps 1-2 word vocabulary + single-payoff structure directly to page spreads, per the vision document — a natural Hindi-feed-launch companion. |
| **Companion app ("Pebble Garden")** | Season 1 complete (100 episodes) AND Hindi feed established | Channel has reached a scale where ongoing content alone can sustain an app's engagement loop (large, evergreen back-catalog per `bubu_franchise_vision.md` §5) | Explicitly the **highest-effort, last-sequenced** item per the vision document — only pursued once everything else above has de-risked the brand. |

# 11. First-Year Roadmap

All subscriber/content targets are **planning calibration points**, deliberately conservative for a new niche channel, and should be read alongside Section 1's qualitative success criteria — a month that hits its subscriber number but shows declining returning-viewer share is **not** a successful month.

## Month 1 — Launch & Stabilize

- **Content:** Public launch with 20-episode inventory (Section 2's "ideal" tier — all of Arc 1, Episodes 1-20). Cadence ramps from 3/week → daily by end of month (Section 3). Production of Episodes 21+ begins on launch day.
- **Subscribers:** **1,000-5,000.** At this stage, absolute numbers matter less than establishing a *baseline* for Section 5's week-over-week growth tracking.
- **Focus:** Every published episode passes `production/quality_checklist.md` at 90+ with **zero exceptions** — Month 1 is where the production pipeline proves it can sustain the quality bar at real cadence. Dashboard (Section 5) is fully operational by end of month.

## Month 3 — Arc 1 Complete, Arc 2 Underway

- **Content:** Approximately Episodes 1-35 published (all of Arc 1, more than half of Arc 2's Colors/Shapes focus). Daily cadence sustained.
- **Subscribers:** **15,000-40,000.** First "Hit Episode" classifications (Section 6) expected by this point, likely from Counting or Water Play episodes (the easiest pillars to make instantly legible, per `content_pillars.md`'s generation-ease ranking).
- **Focus:** First full month of returning-viewer data — this is the first point at which the "most trusted" positioning (Section 1, layer 2) becomes measurable rather than aspirational. The "Pebble Hill Story" playlist should have enough entries (Eps 3, 5, 8, 17, plus Arc 2's Pebble Hill milestone at Ep 33) to function as a real continuity thread.

## Month 6 — Arc 3 Complete, Arc 4 (Hindi) Begins

- **Content:** Approximately Episodes 1-65 published — Arc 3 (Nature/Environment, including the first comfort/fear episode at Ep 55) complete, Arc 4 underway with the first Hindi-word episode (Ep 61, "Pani, Splash!") published.
- **Subscribers:** **75,000-150,000.** First long-form 10-minute compilation (Section 8) becomes viable around this point if the ≥30-Shorts-backlog and growth-trend triggers are both met.
- **Focus:** Section 9's Hindi-word retention check begins — this is the most important *qualitative* checkpoint of the year, since it determines whether the dedicated Hindi feed (Section 10) becomes a Year 2 priority. The first comfort/fear episode (Ep 55) and its resolution pattern should also be reviewed against `production/quality_checklist.md` Section B with particular care, since "nothing stays scary" (World Rule #5) is the highest-stakes rule to get right on its first real use.

## Month 12 — Season 1 Complete

- **Content:** All 100 Season 1 episodes published, including Arc 4's bilingual capstone (Ep 80) and Arc 5's Friendship Pond tease (Ep 91-92). 30-minute bedtime compilations (Section 8) viable if the ≥60-Shorts and Ep-55-aired triggers are met.
- **Subscribers:** **250,000-500,000.** This range assumes the Section 9 Hindi gates were met around Month 6-8, enabling a dedicated Hindi feed to begin contributing to growth in the back half of the year — without that, the lower end of the range is more realistic.
- **Focus:** End-of-Season Outcome (per `season_01_master_roadmap.md` §7) should be demonstrably true: Bubu's full signature set is proven across 100 episodes, Pebble Hill's growth is a visible multi-episode story, Hindi has been layered in without brand confusion, and the franchise has its first real evidence for **Section 10's** merchandise and companion-app triggers. Year 2 planning (Season 2 roadmap, first merchandise line, Hindi feed if not yet launched) begins in Month 12, not after.

## Closing Note

Every section of this plan is reversible *except* the quality bar in Section 1.
